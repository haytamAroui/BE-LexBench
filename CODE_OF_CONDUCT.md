# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the **be-lexbench** project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the integrity of the benchmark
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Attempting to game or contaminate the benchmark (publishing item contents, training on evaluation data, submitting fraudulent scores)
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behaviour that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other forums — and also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by contacting the maintainers directly through GitHub — open a private [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/BE-LexBench/be-lexbench/discussions) marked as a sensitive topic, or use the "Report content" feature on the offending content. All complaints will be reviewed promptly and investigated fairly. Reports will be kept confidential.
